Introduction to chess

Chess is one of the oldest and most intellectually challenging board games in the world. Played by millions of people across all ages and cultures, it is often described as both an art and a science, combining deep strategy, creativity, and psychological insight.

Chess originated in India around the 6th century as a game called chaturanga. It spread through Persia and the Islamic world before reaching Europe, where it evolved into its modern form by the 15th century. Since then, chess has become a symbol of intelligence, discipline, and strategy.

Today, chess is played in tournaments, online platforms, and classrooms worldwide. The rise of computers and artificial intelligence has transformed chess study, allowing players to analyze millions of possible positions. Legendary figures such as Magnus Carlsen, Garry Kasparov, and Bobby Fischer have elevated chess to global fame, inspiring new generations to take up the game.

Beyond competition, chess is also a tool for education and cognitive development, helping improve memory, concentration, problem-solving, and decision-making skills.
